Is Money Evil or Energy? Let’s Break the Spell

For years, I bought into it. Spirituality told me poverty was virtuous. That if I really “transcended,” I wouldn’t care about money. That true enlightenment meant embracing lack, letting go of material desires, and being at peace with just enough.

They almost had me fooled. And maybe they’ve had you fooled too.

But here's the truth they don't want you to hear: money isn’t evil.

It’s neutral. It’s energy.

The problem isn’t money—it’s the programming that’s been embedded in us since birth. That money is for the greedy. That it’s wrong to want it. That if you have too much, you’re "corrupt" or "materialistic."

They’ve taught us that being poor makes us “humble.” That somehow, our struggle is what makes us “spiritual.” But the only thing poverty teaches you is submission and dependence. It keeps you small, sick, and desperate. It feeds the system that benefits from your silence and your struggle.

Let’s break that spell, once and for all.

Spirituality Almost Fooled Me

For the longest time, I wore poverty like a badge of honor. I thought it made me “humble,” “pure,” even “spiritually evolved.” It was a twisted form of pride, built on the lie that suffering is the key to salvation.

I spent so much time focusing on the internal—thinking that money was a distraction or that it somehow lowered my frequency—that I didn’t realize money could actually be a tool for my expansion.

But when I started making extra income, the truth hit me like a lightning bolt. I could help people in ways I never could before. I could offer them opportunities, create jobs, build things that matter. I could take care of myself in ways that didn’t require sacrifice. I could invest in my healing—mentally, physically, emotionally. I could travel and connect with people on a deeper level. I could create spaces where people could expand and awaken too.

And guess what? Money didn’t make me evil.

It made me powerful. And not in the way you’ve been conditioned to fear.

Money is Neutral

Let’s stop playing this game of demonizing what we need to thrive.

Money is a neutral force—it’s just energy. It’s a medium of exchange. A resource. Just like water, air, or food.

What makes money dangerous isn’t the money itself—it’s the energy we attach to it. It’s the greed, the fear, the scarcity mindset that distorts the flow of money. Money in the hands of a conscious, awakened person can be a vehicle for healing, freedom, and transformation. But in the hands of someone who is still shackled by fear and control? It can be weaponized to manipulate, dominate, and destroy.

The Truth About Money in the Current World

Listen closely—whether you like it or not, we live in a world that is driven by money. It’s not just about buying things. It’s about opportunity. Access. Freedom. The ability to create change.

In this reality, we need money. And we need to start seeing it for what it truly is: an instrument of possibility. It’s not a curse. It’s a tool.

If you’re waiting for the world to fix itself without your contribution, think again. The world won’t shift unless we engage with it, and the current system operates on money. Whether you’re building businesses, investing in projects that align with your values, or creating change in the world, you need resources.

Here’s the raw truth: If you’re not focused on gaining wealth, you’re limiting your ability to influence, to help, to grow.

The problem isn’t the pursuit of money; it’s the blind pursuit of it. The obsession with accumulating wealth for the sake of status or superficial gain. The people who treat money like a game to be won at any cost—they are the ones who corrupt it.

But we are not those people.

We understand that money is a tool for freedom, for creativity, for evolution. It’s the key to breaking down walls, breaking out of the rat race, and building something that matters. Something real.

Money is Energy—So Why Not Harness It?

The energy of money, like any other energy, flows to where it’s directed. When you align your purpose with the energy of money, it becomes an ally.

You can use money to fuel your creative vision. You can use money to support your tribe, to elevate others, to make a difference. You can use money to expand your capacity for love, for service, for healing.

So don’t be afraid of it. Don’t push it away. Don’t buy into the lie that wealth equals corruption. Understand that wealth in itself is neutral. It’s your relationship with it that makes all the difference.

And if you’re stuck in the mindset that wealth is for someone else, that it’s “too hard,” or that it’s “not spiritual,” then you’re missing the point. The real spiritual act is expansion, abundance, and creation.

You’re here to live fully—so start living fully. Embrace your power. Stop rejecting the very thing that could help you manifest your greatest desires.

The Truth: You Were Meant to Thrive

You weren’t meant to scrape by. You weren’t meant to feel guilty for wanting more. You were meant to thrive, to create, to expand.

The truth is, money isn’t evil. It’s just energy. And when you shift your perspective, you’ll see it for what it truly is: a means to achieve what you came here to do.

So stop rejecting money. Stop treating it like something to be feared. It’s time to reclaim your right to wealth, freedom, and power. Use it wisely. Use it with intention. Use it to elevate yourself and the world around you.

And never forget: the ones who change the world are the ones who dare to rise.

Your Challenge: Start Expanding

I dare you to look at your relationship with money. What are you telling yourself about it? What are you believing?

Is money the thing that stands between you and your full potential? Or is it the key to unlocking your power, your purpose, your vision?

Stop fearing what’s here to set you free. Let go of the chains. Embrace the flow.

Because you were never meant to live in lack. You were meant to rise.
